## Task Instructions

### Phase 1: Problem Analysis (30 minutes)

**Step 1: Understand Your Context (10 minutes)**
- Read your problem description carefully
- Identify key stakeholders and their interests
- Discuss the decision context and constraints
- Clarify the time horizon and scope

**Example Questions to Discuss:**
- Who are the main decision makers?
- What are the budget constraints?
- What are the environmental regulations?
- What is the timeline for implementation?
- Are there any political or social considerations?

**Step 2: Refine Alternatives and Criteria (15 minutes)**
- Review the provided alternatives - are they complete and appropriate?
- Examine the criteria - do they capture all important aspects?
- Consider if any alternatives or criteria should be added/modified
- Ensure criteria are independent and measurable

**Criteria Quality Check:**
- ✓ **Complete:** Do they cover all important aspects?
- ✓ **Measurable:** Can we assign meaningful scores?
- ✓ **Independent:** Do criteria overlap or double-count?
- ✓ **Relevant:** Do they matter for the decision?

**Step 3: Discuss Weighting Approach (5 minutes)**
- What are the most important criteria for your stakeholders?
- How should you determine weights? (equal, expert judgment, group consensus)
- Consider different stakeholder perspectives
- Document your weighting rationale

**Weighting Exercise:**
1. Each group member individually assigns weights
2. Discuss differences and reasoning
3. Reach consensus through discussion
4. Document final weights and rationale

### Phase 2: MCDM Application (90 minutes)

**Step 1: Access the MCDM Learning Platform (10 minutes)**
- Navigate to the platform URL provided
- Select your assigned problem from the examples
- Familiarize yourself with the interface
- Explore the different tabs (Data Input, Results, Visualizations, Learn)

**Step 2: Set Up Your Analysis (20 minutes)**
- Load your problem data
- Verify/modify alternatives and criteria as discussed
- Input or adjust criterion weights based on group consensus
- Specify criterion types (benefit/cost)

**Important Notes:**
- **Benefit criteria:** Higher values are better (e.g., efficiency, reliability)
- **Cost criteria:** Lower values are better (e.g., cost, emissions)
- **Weights must sum to 1.0**

**Step 3: Apply Multiple Methods (40 minutes)**
- Apply at least 2 different MCDM methods (recommended: SAW + one other)
- Document the rationale for method selection
- Record intermediate results and calculations
- Compare rankings across methods

**Method Selection Guide:**
- **Start with SAW:** Simple and intuitive
- **Add WPM:** If you have mixed units or want multiplicative effects
- **Try TOPSIS:** For robust ranking with ideal/anti-ideal comparison
- **Use AHP:** If you have qualitative criteria or complex stakeholder input

**Step 4: Document Everything (20 minutes)**
- Record all assumptions made during analysis
- Note data sources and estimation methods
- Document any modifications to the original problem
- Screenshot key results for your presentation

**Documentation Template:**
```
Problem: [Brief description]
Stakeholders: [Key decision makers]
Alternatives: [List with brief descriptions]
Criteria: [List with units and benefit/cost designation]
Weights: [Values and rationale]
Methods Used: [Which methods and why]
Key Assumptions: [Important assumptions made]
Data Sources: [Where data came from]
```

### Phase 3: Analysis and Validation (75 minutes)

**Step 1: Sensitivity Analysis**
- Test how changes in weights affect rankings
- Identify critical criteria that strongly influence results
- Examine the stability of your top-ranked alternative
- Document sensitivity findings

**Step 2: Method Comparison**
- Compare results from different MCDM methods
- Analyze any differences in rankings
- Discuss which method seems most appropriate for your problem
- Consider the implications of method choice

**Step 3: Group Validation**
- Do the results make intuitive sense to your group?
- Are there any surprising or counterintuitive findings?
- Would you recommend the top-ranked alternative to stakeholders?
- What additional information would improve the analysis?

**Step 4: Prepare Recommendations**
- Formulate clear recommendations based on your analysis
- Identify implementation considerations
- Suggest areas for further study
- Prepare key messages for presentation

### Common Issues and Solutions

**Problem: Weights don't sum to 1.0**
- Solution: Adjust weights in the sidebar until they sum exactly to 1.0
- Tip: Start with round numbers (0.3, 0.4, 0.3) then fine-tune

**Problem: Unexpected rankings**
- Check criterion types (benefit vs. cost) - this is the most common error
- Verify your data entry - look for typos or wrong units
- Review your weights - small changes can have big impacts

**Problem: Methods give very different results**
- This is normal! Different methods emphasize different aspects
- SAW uses linear aggregation, WPM uses multiplicative
- TOPSIS considers ideal solutions, AHP captures preferences
- Document and discuss the differences

**Problem: Platform won't load or calculate**
- Refresh your browser
- Check internet connection
- Try a different browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari)
- Ask instructor for help

### Advanced Features
- Export results to CSV for further analysis

- Compare multiple scenarios by changing weights
- Access method documentation in Learn tab
