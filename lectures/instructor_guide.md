# MCDM for Renewable Energy: Complete Instructor's Guide

## Course Overview

This comprehensive guide provides everything needed to deliver a successful one-day course on Multi-Criteria Decision Making (MCDM) for renewable energy planning. The course combines theoretical foundations with practical application through hands-on group work.

## Course Structure

### Morning Sessions (3 hours)
- **Session 1:** MCDM Fundamentals (1 hour)
- **Session 2:** MCDM Methods (1 hour)  
- **Session 3:** Implementation & Practice (1 hour)

### Afternoon Session (4 hours)
- **Group Work:** Hands-on MCDM application (3.5 hours)
- **Presentations:** Group results sharing (30 minutes)

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, participants will be able to:

1. **Understand MCDM fundamentals** and their application to renewable energy decisions
2. **Apply four key MCDM methods** (SAW, WPM, TOPSIS, AHP) to real problems
3. **Select appropriate methods** based on problem characteristics and constraints
4. **Perform sensitivity analysis** to test the robustness of decisions
5. **Communicate MCDM results** effectively to stakeholders
6. **Identify common pitfalls** and implement quality assurance measures

## Session-by-Session Delivery Guide

### Session 1: MCDM Fundamentals (60 minutes)

**Objectives:** Introduce MCDM concepts, explain why it's crucial for renewable energy, cover five key components

**Key Teaching Points:**
- Start with relatable examples (car buying) before energy examples
- Emphasize the "no perfect solution" challenge
- Use concrete data in examples (Austin city scenario)
- Interactive elements: Ask "Which would you choose?" questions
- Connect to afternoon group work throughout

**Timing Breakdown:**
- Introduction & overview: 5 minutes
- What is MCDM: 8 minutes
- Real-world example: 10 minutes
- Five components: 30 minutes
- Case study preview: 5 minutes
- Summary & transition: 2 minutes

**Common Questions & Answers:**
- **Q:** "Isn't this just common sense?"
- **A:** "Yes, but systematic common sense! MCDM makes implicit thinking explicit and transparent."

- **Q:** "How do you handle politics in real decisions?"
- **A:** "MCDM doesn't eliminate politics, but it makes the basis for decisions transparent and defensible."

### Session 2: MCDM Methods (60 minutes)

**Objectives:** Teach four specific methods with calculations, show when to use each method

**Key Teaching Points:**
- Work through SAW calculation step-by-step on board
- Emphasize WPM's dimensionless advantage with concrete examples
- Use visual aids for TOPSIS geometric interpretation
- Show how AHP handles qualitative criteria
- Compare method results to show they can differ

**Timing Breakdown:**
- Methods overview: 5 minutes
- SAW with calculation: 15 minutes
- WPM with calculation: 15 minutes
- TOPSIS concepts: 10 minutes
- AHP concepts: 10 minutes
- Method comparison: 5 minutes

**Teaching Tips:**
- Use same data across methods to show differences
- Have students calculate along for SAW example
- Draw TOPSIS diagram on board for visual learners
- Emphasize that different results are normal and expected

### Session 3: Implementation & Practice (60 minutes)

**Objectives:** Prepare students for practical application, demonstrate platform, set up group work

**Key Teaching Points:**
- Implementation is iterative with feedback loops
- Problem definition is most critical phase
- Live platform demonstration is essential
- Common pitfalls help avoid mistakes
- Clear group work instructions prevent confusion

**Timing Breakdown:**
- Implementation framework: 10 minutes
- Software tools: 10 minutes
- Platform demonstration: 15 minutes
- Common pitfalls: 10 minutes
- Group work preparation: 15 minutes

**Platform Demonstration Script:**
1. Show example problem selection
2. Demonstrate data input and editing
3. Apply SAW method and show results
4. Show sensitivity analysis features
5. Demonstrate download functionality

## Group Work Management

### Group Formation (13:00-13:30)

**Process:**
1. Ask participants to form groups of 4-5 people
2. Assign problems to groups (or let them choose)
3. Distribute printed materials to each group
4. Ensure each group has platform access
5. Circulate to answer initial questions

**Problem Assignment Strategy:**
- Let groups choose if possible (increases engagement)
- Ensure even distribution across problems
- Consider participant backgrounds when assigning
- Have backup problems ready for large classes

### Monitoring Progress (13:30-16:30)

**Instructor Role:**
- Circulate among groups every 15-20 minutes
- Answer technical questions about platform
- Help with method selection decisions
- Provide guidance on weight determination
- Assist with interpretation of results

**Common Issues & Solutions:**
- **Platform not loading:** Use backup Excel templates
- **Weights don't sum to 1.0:** Show how to adjust in platform
- **Unexpected rankings:** Help check criterion types (benefit/cost)
- **Method disagreement:** Explain this is normal, focus on insights
- **Time management:** Provide gentle reminders of schedule

### Presentation Management (16:30-17:00)

**Setup:**
- Strict 5-minute time limit per group
- 2-3 minutes for questions after each presentation
- Use timer visible to all groups
- Encourage use of platform screenshots

**Evaluation Focus:**
- Problem understanding and stakeholder identification
- Appropriate method selection and application
- Quality of sensitivity analysis
- Practical relevance of recommendations
- Clear communication of results

## Troubleshooting Guide

### Technical Issues
- **Platform down:** Use Excel templates and manual calculations
- **Internet problems:** Pre-download platform screenshots for demo
- **Projection issues:** Have backup laptop ready
- **Audio problems:** Use voice projection techniques

### Pedagogical Challenges
- **Participants with strong math anxiety:** Emphasize concepts over calculations
- **Diverse technical backgrounds:** Pair experienced with novice participants
- **Time running short:** Focus on key concepts, skip detailed calculations
- **Low engagement:** Use more interactive questions and examples

### Group Dynamics
- **Dominant personalities:** Encourage equal participation
- **Quiet participants:** Create structured roles within groups
- **Conflicting opinions:** Frame as learning opportunity about stakeholder differences
- **Off-task behavior:** Redirect to practical applications
